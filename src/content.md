service2Title: Line Stopping
service2Tag: Core Service
service2Body1: Line stopping is a method of temporarily isolating a section of live pipeline to allow maintenance, repairs, or modifications — without a full system shutdown. A line stop plug is inserted through a hot tap fitting and expanded to block flow.
service2Body2: Once the work is complete, the plug is retracted and the pipeline returns to full operation. The fitting and valve remain in place as a permanent asset.
service2li1: Temporary flow isolation without system shutdown
service2li2: Enables repairs and modifications on live lines
service2li3: Plug retracted after work — full flow restored
service2li4: Fitting and valve retained as permanent asset
